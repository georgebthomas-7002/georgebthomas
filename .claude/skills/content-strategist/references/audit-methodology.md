# Content Audit Methodology

Detailed evaluation criteria, scoring rubrics, and common problems for content auditing.

## The 5-Point Scoring System

Use consistently across all criteria for standardized assessment.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Excellent | Follows best practices, meets user needs fully, no changes needed |
| 4 | Good | Minor issues, generally effective, polish improvements only |
| 3 | Average | Functional but needs improvement, clear opportunities |
| 2 | Poor | Multiple problems, confusing for users, significant revision needed |
| 1 | Critical | Causes significant problems, needs immediate overhaul |

---

## Detailed Scoring Rubrics

### Criterion 1: Headline & Hook Effectiveness

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Passes 2-second comprehension test
- Promises specific, desirable outcome
- Uses customer language (not company jargon)
- Creates curiosity or urgency appropriately
- Front-loads most important words

**Score 4 - Good**
- Clear meaning but could be more compelling
- Benefit present but not strongly stated
- Minor word choice improvements possible

**Score 3 - Average**
- Understandable but generic
- Could apply to competitors
- Missing specific value promise
- Doesn't speak to pain point

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Unclear or confusing
- Company-focused instead of customer-focused
- Buried value proposition
- Requires context to understand

**Score 1 - Critical**
- Meaningless or misleading
- Actively repels target audience
- No connection to page content
- Generic placeholder language

**Common Headline Problems:**
- "Welcome to [Company Name]" - says nothing
- Feature-first instead of benefit-first
- Too clever/cute at expense of clarity
- Missing "what's in it for me"
- Too long (over 10 words for main headlines)

---

### Criterion 2: Value Proposition Clarity

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Immediately clear what's offered and to whom
- Unique differentiator evident
- Written from customer perspective
- Answers "why you?" convincingly
- Passes the 5-second test

**Score 4 - Good**
- Clear offering but differentiation soft
- Customer-focused but could be sharper
- Value present but not compelling

**Score 3 - Average**
- Takes more than 5 seconds to understand
- Could apply to multiple competitors
- Mix of customer/company focus
- Generic benefit language

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Unclear what's actually offered
- Company-centric language dominates
- No differentiation from alternatives
- Jargon obscures meaning

**Score 1 - Critical**
- Cannot determine value proposition
- Actively confusing
- Contradictory messages
- No clear offering

**Value Proposition Test Questions:**
1. What do they do?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why is it better/different?
4. What outcome will I get?

If any answer takes more than 5 seconds to find, there's a problem.

---

### Criterion 3: Structure & Scanability

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Clear heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- Paragraphs under 100 words
- Front-loaded keywords in headings
- Adequate white space
- One idea per paragraph
- Scannable narrative through headings alone

**Score 4 - Good**
- Good structure with minor inconsistencies
- Mostly scannable
- Some paragraphs run long
- Hierarchy generally clear

**Score 3 - Average**
- Inconsistent heading usage
- Some walls of text
- Ideas blend across paragraphs
- Headings don't tell story alone

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Minimal or no headings
- Dense text blocks
- Poor visual hierarchy
- Difficult to scan
- Information hard to locate

**Score 1 - Critical**
- No structure
- Single massive text block
- Impossible to scan
- Information architecture absent

**Scanability Checklist:**
- [ ] H1 is clear page title
- [ ] H2s break content into logical sections
- [ ] H3s used appropriately (not skipping levels)
- [ ] No paragraph over 100 words
- [ ] Keywords front-loaded in headings
- [ ] Headings make sense read sequentially
- [ ] Adequate white space between sections
- [ ] Visual breaks every 300-400 words

---

### Criterion 4: Proof & Credibility

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Proof-to-claim ratio of 2:1 or better
- Specific results with numbers
- Credible testimonials (name, photo, title)
- Third-party validation where relevant
- Every major claim has evidence within 150 words

**Score 4 - Good**
- Most claims supported
- Good testimonials but could be more specific
- Some proof points missing context
- Generally credible

**Score 3 - Average**
- Claims outpace proof
- Generic testimonials ("Great company!")
- Missing specificity in results
- Some unsubstantiated claims

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Many unproven claims
- No or weak testimonials
- Vague results ("improved outcomes")
- Trust signals absent

**Score 1 - Critical**
- All claims unsubstantiated
- No social proof
- Potentially misleading statements
- Credibility actively damaged

**Proof Types (Strongest to Weakest):**
1. Specific results with numbers and context
2. Named testimonials with specifics
3. Third-party validation (awards, certifications)
4. Client logos
5. General statistics
6. Unnamed testimonials
7. Claims without evidence

**Proof-to-Claim Ratio Calculation:**
- Count distinct claims made
- Count distinct proof points provided
- Divide claims by proofs
- Ideal: 2.0 or lower (no more than 2 claims per proof)
- On crucial pages (pricing, about, services): aim for 1:1

---

### Criterion 5: Voice & Tone

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Consistent voice throughout
- Appropriate for target audience
- "You" focused (not "we" focused)
- Matches brand personality
- Natural, not robotic or corporate

**Score 4 - Good**
- Generally consistent
- Mostly audience-appropriate
- Minor inconsistencies
- Occasional "we" heavy sections

**Score 3 - Average**
- Inconsistent across sections
- Generic corporate voice
- Mix of formal/informal
- Neither distinctly good nor bad

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Jarring inconsistencies
- Wrong tone for audience
- Overly formal or too casual
- "We" dominated throughout

**Score 1 - Critical**
- Multiple conflicting voices
- Completely wrong for audience
- Unprofessional or inappropriate
- Damages brand perception

**Voice/Tone Quick Audit:**
- Count "you/your" vs "we/our" in first 3 paragraphs
- Ideal ratio: 2:1 or higher in favor of "you"
- Read aloud: does it sound like a person talking?
- Would target audience use this language?

---

### Criterion 6: CTA Effectiveness

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Clear, specific action requested
- Value communicated in CTA
- High contrast, easy to find
- Friction minimized
- Multiple appropriate opportunities
- Primary and transitional options available

**Score 4 - Good**
- Clear CTA present
- Could be more compelling
- Good placement but not optimal
- Minor friction present

**Score 3 - Average**
- CTA present but generic ("Learn More")
- Not strongly connected to value
- Placement buried or unclear
- Only one CTA option

**Score 2 - Poor**
- Weak or unclear CTA
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Hard to find
- High friction (long forms, unclear process)

**Score 1 - Critical**
- No clear CTA
- Contradictory CTAs
- Broken or non-functional
- Completely unclear next steps

**CTA Best Practices:**
- Action verb + value ("Get Your Free Audit" vs "Submit")
- High contrast button color
- Above the fold AND repeated
- Form fields: minimum necessary
- Transitional CTA for those not ready

## Common Content Problems by Category

### Headline & Hook Problems

| Problem | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Welcome mat | "Welcome to ABC Corp" | Lead with value proposition |
| Feature-first | "AI-Powered Platform" | "Close deals 40% faster" |
| Jargon heavy | "Omnichannel synergy solutions" | "Reach customers everywhere" |
| Too clever | "Think Outside the Inbox" | State benefit clearly first |
| Too vague | "Better Results" | "2X your conversion rate" |

### Structure Problems

| Problem | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Wall of text | 300+ word paragraphs | Break into 50-100 word chunks |
| Heading soup | Too many headings | Consolidate related sections |
| Flat hierarchy | All H2s, no H3s | Create logical nesting |
| Buried info | Key details in middle | Front-load important content |
| No scanpath | Headings don't tell story | Rewrite headings as narrative |

### Messaging Problems

| Problem | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Feature dump | List of product features | Transform to benefits |
| We/our focus | Company-centric language | Rewrite with "you" |
| Generic claims | "Industry-leading" | Add specific proof |
| Missing stakes | No consequence shown | Add "without this..." |
| Unclear audience | Could be for anyone | Specify who it's for |

### CTA Problems

| Problem | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Generic button | "Learn More" | "Get Your Free Strategy Session" |
| Competing CTAs | 3 equal buttons | Establish clear primary |
| Hidden CTA | Below fold, low contrast | Above fold, high contrast |
| High friction | 15-field form | Reduce to essentials |
| No transitional | Only "Buy Now" | Add "Download Guide" option |

## Priority Classification System

### P0 - Critical (Fix Immediately)
- Unclear value proposition on high-traffic pages
- Missing or broken CTAs on conversion pages
- Wrong audience targeting
- Actively misleading content
- Revenue-impacting issues

### P1 - High (Fix Within Current Scope)
- Weak headlines on important pages
- Feature-focused instead of benefit-focused
- Walls of text hurting engagement
- Missing social proof
- Poor mobile experience

### P2 - Medium (Next Quarter)
- Voice inconsistencies
- Missing proof points
- Suboptimal structure
- Internal link improvements
- Moderate overlap issues

### P3 - Low (As Resources Allow)
- Minor clarity improvements
- Formatting polish
- Evergreen content refreshes
- Lower-traffic page updates

---

## Impact-Effort Matrix

Prioritize based on:

**High Impact / Low Effort (Do First)**
- Headline rewrites
- CTA optimization
- Above-fold improvements

**High Impact / High Effort (Plan For)**
- Full page restructures
- Comprehensive messaging overhaul
- New section creation

**Low Impact / Low Effort (Quick Wins)**
- Formatting fixes
- Minor word changes
- Image optimization

**Low Impact / High Effort (Avoid)**
- Marginal improvements to low-traffic pages
- Perfectionism on minor details
